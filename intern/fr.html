How to get started?


A. Identifying Areas of Interest

• Most of you are beginning your third year now and would have a decent idea on what you are interested in. If not, identify a field in which you’d want to do your internship in.

• Look at the projects you’ve done and courses you’ve undertaken as well as your personal favourite topics for choosing an area of interest.

• If you’ve done a previous internship, judge yourself as to whether that’s a field you like and would like to go ahead with.

• It’s not important that you have to apply for an internship in fields where you did well in your course or an area where you’ve done a project already but it is better to apply in fields you have worked in as your application stands a better chance to be accepted.


B. Getting your Resume ready

• Your resume is the most important document for you over your college period. You sell yourself to a company or a university professor with it and hence, you must spend ample time on making your resume look good. (This does not mean you fill it up with nonsense!)

• Get your resume ready at the earliest because you need to send it along with your application cover mail.

• There isn’t any specific template that makes your resume good-looking. Choose a template of your choice. You can see a lot of example resumes online. A document containing a plethora of sample resumes will be shared with you guys.

• DO NOT Lie in your resume. Trust me, that is the last thing you want to do.


C. Creating a Database of Contacts

• This is the next important step after your resume is ready. Finding relevant professors is not very difficult but it is a time-consuming task. Easily one can take a good 10-30 hours finding contacts (if you want a decent database).

• Go through QS Rankings for having an idea about the Top Universities. Again, have an idea about your resume and your skills before applying to the extreme top universities as these mostly need a stellar resume.

• Create a spreadsheet with the Headings: Professor Name, University, Email ID, Area of research, Link and Status.

• After having an idea about universities to apply, go through their web pages and relevant departments. You will get the professor contact there. Some of these pages are difficult to navigate due to poor/ complicated UI.

• You can also Google with the university and its relevant department name to go straight to that webpage.

• A point to note is to collect Professor contacts rather than PostDoc and Research Scholars.

• Collect as many contacts as possible because getting replies is very difficult. There are people who have sent 100 mails and have not got any response.


D. Writing a good Cover Letter

• Cover Letter is what you’ll be sending as an e-mail to the respective professors. A good cover letter is very important as that’s what makes your mail stand out from the 100 such internship requests that a professor gets every day.

• The Cover Letter should be short and concise with good formatting. It should also be grammatically sound.

• A good cover letter format would be the following.
I. First Paragraph - Introduction and purpose of writing
II. Second Paragraph - Your skills and interests
III. Third Paragraph - How are your skills relevant to the position you are applying for
IV. Fourth Paragraph - Humble request to accept and greetings

• The above given format is not hard and fixed. You may use a format of your choice. The paragraphs may also be split into smaller ones. Make sure that what you write is genuine, polite and to the point.

• Reading through the professor’s Research work as well as including details about it in the mail is very important as it shows your interest. For e.g. You may write as if you are interested in a particular project and would like to work together with the professor or you could give your ideas on how to extend the prof’s work.

• Have a common cover letter ready so that you easily access it and change just the relevant points according to the Professor and University.


E. Mailing the Professor

• Once your database and cover letter are ready, you can start mailing!

• The best times to mail are from Monday to Thursday (between 10am and 12pm Local Time). Schedule your mail accordingly. Check for any national holidays in that country and try to avoid mailing on those days

• As and when you mail, update the Status in your spreadsheet

• There are mixed opinions about using personal ID or IIT Id or emailing but I had success with IIT mail and had no issues. So, I’d say, that’s your choice. Also, IIT Id might make the receiver read the email.

• Use tools like Mailtrack for tracking your email. Also, if you give a link to your resume using bitly in your email, you can get to know if it has been opened or not.

• Do not SPAM a professor with multiple emails. You may send a followup email after 3-4 days and even after that if there’s no response just leave it be. Our institute has been blacklisted in the past for mass mailing.

• It is always advisable not to mention about funding in your first email. Also, if you are applying for a scholarship program, mention in your email that you are applying via it for an internship under the professor.

• If you don’t get a response, don’t get disappointed. There are people who have sent 100 emails and not got a single response. So, all you can do is be patient and put in the effort.


F. Timeframe

• Start ASAP on making your resume, cover letter and database. Usually now is the period when everyone sends mails to professors if times were normal. Considering the COVID situation, things might get delayed next year (including Scholarship Programs). As of now, even we are in the blue about things.

• Start sending mails in this month (August) itself (earlier the better) since there are programs like DAAD-WISE which close by 1 November. Also, usually all the good internships at TOP Universities get filled up soon so better to start mailing early.

• After you get the response, you need to spend a lot of time on other procedures if you are applying via a scholarship program. These include, writing SOP’s, filling forms, Getting documents ready etc. Easily, one month will be required for all this.


G. Funding

• Your expenditure is going to vary according to the region of the University (with places like the US and UK tending to be on the more expensive side and Asian countries slightly cheaper)

• A rough average of 60,000 INR would be required for expenses per month (main ones being Accommodation, Food and travelling). This is a very vague average and would vary highly depending on the region. You can apply for scholarship programs most of which provide enough money to cover all the expenditure.

• If you are not applying for Scholarship, you may ask the professor if he/she would provide a stipend but avoid doing so in your first mail. This can be done once you get approval for an internship and establish good communication with the professor. Sometimes a professor might give you some kind of stipend once your work is done if it is satisfactory.


H. Miscellaneous

• Get a Passport if you don’t have one already. This takes a good month hence apply for one ASAP.

• After getting an internship, you will have to apply for a VISA. Take care of the type of VISA required for that country for an internship.

• Your best sources for help are final year students who have done this before. A list of students who got foreign internships last year has been attached in the Resources Section.

Why apply for a Foreign Research Internship?

• You get access to quality research going on in the respective fields as well as access to some modern tech.
• You will interact with a lot of people giving you a good exposure in the field.
• Mostly, the internship will be very flexible with no fixed working hours hence giving you a high fun/work ratio.
• You can explore a new country and surrounding countries if possible and thereby do a lot of travel.
• A lot of scholarships and programs are available which will almost entirely fund your expenditure during the internship period. (*Details of the programs are provided separately)
